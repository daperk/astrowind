## Master These Sleep Habits First

Before spending a dime on sleep products, focus on these foundational habits that sleep specialists unanimously recommend:

**Consistency is king.** Your body thrives on routine. Go to bed and wake up at the same time every day—yes, even on weekends. This regulates your circadian rhythm, your body's internal clock that controls the sleep-wake cycle. After just two weeks of consistency, many people report falling asleep faster and waking more refreshed.

**Create a wind-down ritual.** Your brain needs transition time. Establish a 30-minute pre-sleep routine that signals to your body it's time to rest. This might include gentle stretching, reading a physical book (not an e-reader), or practicing meditation. The specific activities matter less than their consistency.

**Ban blue light before bed.** In 2025, this advice is more crucial than ever. New research shows that blue light exposure suppresses melatonin production for up to 50% longer than previously thought. Stop using phones, tablets, computers, and TVs at least one hour before bedtime. If you must use devices, wear blue light blocking glasses, which have become more stylish and effective in 2025.

**Limit daytime naps.** If you nap, keep it under 20 minutes and before 3 PM. The new "NASA nap" technique—drinking a small cup of coffee right before a 20-minute nap—has gained popularity in 2025 for its proven alertness benefits without disrupting nighttime sleep.

**Exercise, but time it right.** Regular physical activity improves sleep quality dramatically, but timing matters. The latest research recommends completing vigorous exercise at least 3-4 hours before bedtime. Morning exercise, particularly outdoors, provides the strongest sleep benefits by reinforcing your circadian rhythm.
