## How to Choose the Perfect Sleep Mask

Selecting the ideal sleep mask depends on your personal preferences and sleeping style. Here are the key factors to consider:

**Sleeping Position:** Side sleepers should prioritize masks with contoured eye cups and cushioned edges like the **Alaska Bear** or **LitBear**. Back sleepers have more flexibility but may prefer masks with secure straps to prevent shifting.

**Skin Sensitivity:** If you have sensitive skin or are concerned about facial creases, prioritize silk options like the **Quince** or **Alaska Bear**. The natural protein fibers in silk are less likely to cause irritation or absorption of skincare products.

**Light Sensitivity:** For complete blackout, choose masks with nose wings or contoured designs like the **MZOO** or **LitBear** that prevent light leakage around the edges.

**Eyelash Considerations:** If you wear false lashes or extensions, contoured masks that create space for your eyes are essential. The **Alaska Bear** and **MZOO** offer excellent lash protection.

**Budget:** While premium options offer luxury materials, even our budget pick (**LitBear** at $16.99) provides excellent light blocking and comfort.

## How to Care for Your Sleep Mask

Proper maintenance extends the life of your sleep mask and maintains its hygiene and effectiveness:

**Silk Masks:** Hand wash with mild, pH-balanced soap in cool water. Never wring – instead, press between towels to remove excess water, then lay flat to dry. The **Quince** and silk portions of the **Alaska Bear** require this gentle treatment.

**Memory Foam Masks:** Most can be hand washed with mild soap, but never machine washed as this damages the foam structure. The **MZOO** and **LitBear** should be air dried completely before reuse.

**Frequency:** Wash your mask weekly for optimal hygiene. If you apply serums or creams before bed, consider using a protective pillowcase or washing more frequently, as skincare products can break down mask materials over time.

**Storage:** Keep your mask in a clean, dry place. Many premium masks include storage pouches – use these to prevent dust accumulation and maintain cleanliness.
