## Daily Habits: The Foundation of Energy Management

Technology and supplements can support your efforts, but consistent daily habits form the true foundation of energy management. The latest behavioral science points to several key practices.

Light exposure sequencing has emerged as the most impactful daily habit. Researchers now recommend 10-15 minutes of direct morning sunlight within 30 minutes of waking, followed by reduced blue light exposure starting 3 hours before bedtime. This simple practice helps reset your circadian rhythm more effectively than any supplement.

Movement timing matters more than previously understood. Short 5-minute movement breaks every 90 minutes during the day help regulate energy levels, while gentle yoga or stretching 60-90 minutes before bed improves sleep onset. This approach is more effective than a single longer workout at random times.

Caffeine metabolism varies dramatically between individuals, but new research suggests the safest cutoff for most people is 10 hours before bedtime, not the previously recommended 6 hours. This longer window accounts for slow metabolizers who make up about 50% of the population.

Sleep consistency trumps duration. Maintaining the same sleep and wake times (within 30 minutes) even on weekends stabilizes your circadian rhythm. The 2025 consensus recommendation is that consistency matters more than occasionally getting extra sleep.
