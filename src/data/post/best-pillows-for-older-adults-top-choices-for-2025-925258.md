## What Seniors Need in a Pillow

Older adults have specific requirements that differ from younger sleepers. As we age, our bodies change, and so do our sleep needs. The right pillow becomes crucial for maintaining comfort and supporting health.

A high-quality pillow for seniors should offer:

- **Adjustable loft and firmness** to maintain proper spinal alignment
- **Pressure relief** for sensitive areas like the neck and shoulders
- **Easy maintenance** with washable covers
- **Breathability** to prevent overheating during sleep
- **Durability** to maintain shape and support over time
- **Hypoallergenic properties** for those with sensitivities

Most importantly, older adults need pillows that accommodate position changes throughout the night. Many seniors find themselves switching between back, side, and sometimes stomach sleeping, especially those with conditions like arthritis or sleep apnea.

## Special Considerations for Older Adults

When choosing a pillow as a senior, there are several specific factors to consider beyond general comfort:

### Health Conditions

Many older adults deal with health issues that affect sleep quality. If you have arthritis, look for a pillow that's easy to adjust without requiring much hand strength. For those with sleep apnea, a pillow with adjustable height can help maintain proper alignment with CPAP machines.

### Sleep Position Changes

As we age, many of us change positions more frequently during the night. An adjustable pillow that works well for multiple positions can prevent discomfort when shifting from back to side sleeping.

### Temperature Regulation

Many seniors experience changes in temperature sensitivity. Pillows with cooling properties like the Purple Harmony or breathable materials can help prevent overheating and night sweats.

For those interested in more sleep technology, our [best sleep trackers comparison guide](/best-sleep-trackers-comparison-guide) can help you monitor how your new pillow affects your sleep quality.

### Ease of Maintenance

Consider how easy the pillow is to clean and maintain. Pillows with removable, washable covers are ideal, especially for those who may have incontinence concerns or use CPAP machines that can leave oils on pillowcases.
