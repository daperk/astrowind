## Do Weighted Blankets Actually Work?

Weighted blankets are built around a concept called deep pressure stimulation, which is the same idea behind a firm hug or swaddling an infant. The gentle, even pressure is thought to calm the nervous system and help some people feel more relaxed at bedtime.

The honest picture from research is mixed but promising. According to the [Sleep Foundation](https://www.sleepfoundation.org/best-weighted-blankets), studies suggest weighted blankets may help reduce anxiety and improve the feeling of restfulness for certain people, though they are not a cure for insomnia on their own.

Here is who tends to benefit most:

- People who feel anxious or restless when trying to fall asleep
- Sleepers who like the feeling of being tucked in tightly
- Those who move around a lot and want a grounding sensation

And who usually does not love them:

- Hot sleepers who already overheat at night, unless they pick a breathable design
- People with limited mobility who find the weight hard to reposition
- Anyone who feels claustrophobic under heavy bedding

If your sleep issues are more about your room being too warm or bright, a blanket will not fix that. Start with [optimizing your bedroom environment for better sleep](/optimize-bedroom-environment-for-better-sleep) first.

## How to Choose the Right Weight

This is the single most important decision, and most people get it wrong.

The common rule is to pick a blanket that is roughly 10 percent of your body weight. So a 150-pound person would look at a 15-pound blanket. This is a starting point, not a strict law.

- **Go lighter** if you are new to weighted blankets, sleep hot, or want an easier feel.
- **Go heavier** only if you have used one before and want more pressure.
- **For couples,** buy two separate blankets rather than one huge shared one. Large blankets slide the weight toward whoever moves less, which ruins the even pressure.

Kids need special care here. Very heavy blankets are not safe for small children or anyone who cannot easily move the blanket off themselves, so check the manufacturer's guidance before buying for a child.

## Cooling: The Biggest Complaint to Avoid

The number one reason people return weighted blankets is heat. Traditional bead-and-polyester blankets act like an insulator, which is great in winter and miserable in summer.

If you tend to overheat, prioritize a knit design like the Bearaby line or a blanket with a breathable cotton or bamboo cover. Skip anything with a plush minky or fleece side unless your bedroom runs cold.

You can also pair a warmer weighted blanket with cooler bedding underneath. For a full breakdown of gear that helps with temperature and comfort, see our [sleep tools and gadgets review](/best-sleep-tools-and-gadgets-review).

## Care and Longevity

A good weighted blanket should last several years if you treat it right.

- Check the label. Some blankets are fully machine washable, while heavier ones need a commercial-size washer or spot cleaning.
- Use a removable duvet cover on bead blankets to cut down on washing the whole thing.
- Dry on low or air dry. High heat can damage the beads and the stitching over time.

Cheaper blankets tend to show wear faster, with beads shifting or leaking if a seam fails. That is one reason the mid-range options often pay off over the long run.

## FAQ

**Q: What weight weighted blanket should I get?**
Start with roughly 10 percent of your body weight. If you are new to weighted blankets or sleep hot, size down slightly. Only go heavier if you have used one before and want more pressure.

**Q: Are weighted blankets good for anxiety?**
Many people find the deep pressure calming, and some research suggests they may reduce anxiety and improve restfulness. They are a helpful tool for some sleepers, but they are not a medical treatment.

**Q: Do weighted blankets make you sleep hotter?**
Traditional bead blankets can trap heat. If you run warm, choose a breathable knit design or a blanket with a cooling cotton or TENCEL cover.

**Q: Can kids use weighted blankets?**
Only with caution and the right weight. Very young children and anyone who cannot move the blanket off themselves should not use a heavy one. Always follow the manufacturer's age and weight guidance.

**Q: How do I wash a weighted blanket?**
It depends on the model. Some are fully machine washable, while heavier ones need spot cleaning or a large-capacity washer. A removable cover makes cleaning much easier.
