## Sleep Habits That Actually Work

While products can enhance sleep, habits form the foundation. Sleep researchers in 2025 have refined our understanding of which habits deliver the biggest benefits.

Consistency reigns supreme. Going to bed and waking at the same time—even on weekends—remains the single most powerful habit for improving sleep quality. Your circadian rhythm thrives on predictability, regulating hormones like melatonin and cortisol more effectively when on a regular schedule.

The pre-sleep wind-down has been refined in 2025. The ideal routine starts 90 minutes before bed, beginning with completing any stimulating activities, followed by dimming lights and reducing screen exposure 60 minutes pre-bed, and ending with a relaxation activity like the **Bob and Brad EyeOasis 2** eye massager in the final 30 minutes.

Screen management has evolved beyond simple blue light filters. The latest approach incorporates "content scheduling"—avoiding emotionally stimulating or work-related content in the evening hours, not just limiting screen time. Some sleep therapists now recommend scheduling specific "worry time" earlier in the evening to process anxieties before bed.

Nutrition timing shows increasing importance in 2025 research. Finishing dinner 3 hours before bed and limiting liquids in the final 90 minutes reduces nighttime disruptions. For those seeking additional support, **magnesium glycinate** (200-400mg) has emerged as one of the most evidence-backed sleep supplements.

## Sleep Supplements: What's Worth It in 2025

The supplement market continues to grow, but scientific validation remains critical when **seeking sleep advice** about supplements. 2025 research provides clearer guidance on what actually works.

Melatonin remains effective but with new usage guidelines. Micro-dosing (0.5-1mg) is now preferred over the previously common 3-10mg doses, with research showing equal effectiveness with fewer side effects. Time-release formulations better mimic natural melatonin production.

Magnesium has gained stronger scientific backing. Specifically, **magnesium glycinate** shows the most promise for sleep quality improvement at doses of 200-400mg. It works by calming the nervous system and regulating neurotransmitters involved in sleep.

Adaptogens are receiving increased attention in 2025. **Ashwagandha** (300-600mg) helps reduce cortisol levels, particularly beneficial for stress-related sleep issues. Meanwhile, **L-theanine** (200-400mg) promotes relaxation without sedation, making it ideal for those with racing thoughts at bedtime.

CBD research has matured, showing benefits for specific sleep concerns. Full-spectrum CBD oil (25-50mg) appears most effective for anxiety-related sleep problems, while CBN (a different cannabinoid) shows promise specifically for sleep maintenance.

Timing matters significantly. Most supplements should be taken 30-60 minutes before bed, though some adaptogens work better when taken throughout the day to manage overall stress levels that impact sleep.
